# 🧱 Scratch Basics: The Building Blocks

Before we start coding, let's understand the terms we'll be using in Scratch. Think of Scratch as a theater where you are the director!

## 🎭 key Terms

### 1. Sprite
A **Sprite** is an object in your program. It can be a rover, a star, a cat, or anything else. Sprites are the "actors" that perform actions based on the code you write.

### 2. Backdrop
The **Backdrop** is the background image of your **Stage**. It sets the scene. In our project, the backdrop will be the surface of Mars or the Moon.

### 3. Stage
The **Stage** is the area where your program's actions happen. It's the "screen" where you see your sprites move and interact.

### 4. Blocks
**Blocks** are the visual commands you snap together to give instructions. They are like "puzzle pieces" of code. 
- **Events**: "When something happens..." (e.g., *When Green Flag Clicked*)
- **Motion**: "Move like this..."
- **Sensing**: "If you touch this color..."

---

## 🛠️ The Workspace
- **Block Palette**: Where all the blocks live (on the left).
- **Code Area**: Where you drag and snap blocks to create "scripts" (in the middle).
- **Stage window**: Where the game runs (on the right).
- **Sprite List**: Where you manage all your actors (bottom right).

---

> [!TIP]
> **Remember**: The computer will only do *exactly* what you tell it to do, in the *exact* order you snap the blocks together!
